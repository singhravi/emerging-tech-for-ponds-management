Automated technology pipeline
From pond scan to weed-free water — 6 stages
Every mission runs the full sequence autonomously, with zero herbicide and zero fish disturbance.
📸
Stage 01 — Aerial survey
Drone multispectral survey
An autonomous drone flies a pre-programmed grid pattern at 5 m altitude. Onboard 5-band multispectral and RGB cameras capture imagery every 0.5 s, building a complete spatial map of the pond surface. Coverage rate: 1 hectare in under 12 minutes.
🧠
Stage 02 — Computer vision
AI species classification
Edge-deployed YOLOv8-nano detects objects in real time; EfficientNet-B2 classifies each region to species level. The model distinguishes Makhana pads, Singhara, three invasive weed species, open water, and fish — all within 80 ms per frame on the onboard Jetson Orin.
📍
Stage 03 — Mission planning
Weed coordinate extraction & queue
Detected bounding boxes are converted to GPS coordinates using camera calibration and drone telemetry. A priority queue is built — densest weed clusters are cleared first to maximise crop light exposure as quickly as possible.
🋔
Stage 04 — Robot deployment
Pontoon robot navigation
The floating pontoon robot receives the weed coordinate queue via 900 MHz LoRa mesh. It navigates using GPS and ultrasonic obstacle avoidance, decelerating within designated fish zones to avoid any disturbance to aquatic life.
🦾
Stage 05 — Extraction
Mechanical weed extraction
A 6-DOF arm with soft-grip end effector grasps each weed stem at root level and extracts it completely. A force-torque sensor caps grip pressure at 2.5 N — preventing any disturbance to fish or adjacent crop plants. Extracted biomass is collected in an onboard bin for composting.
📊
Stage 06 — Reporting
Analytics & farmer reporting
All operations are logged to a cloud dashboard in real time. Weed density heatmaps, clearance rates, robot path efficiency, and seasonal yield projections are auto-generated after each mission. Farmers receive a WhatsApp summary report within minutes of mission completion.
